What is Semantics HTML tags

Simply put, a semantic element clearly describes its meaning to both the browser and developer - for example, instead of div and span, we can use better alternatives such as form, table, and img. Interesting trivia: The HTML5 WG came up with semantics such as header, footer, nav, section after Google analyzed 1 billion pages to see what authors were using to describe their divs and other elements.
Fight against divitis! Instead of div’s everywhere with ID’s and classes, use semantic HTML5 tags. Why use semantic tags? Primarily to enhace: accessibility - help assistive technologies read and interpret your webpage searchability - help computers make sense of your content internationalization - only 13% of the world are English native speakers interoperability - help other programmers understand the structure of your webpage
